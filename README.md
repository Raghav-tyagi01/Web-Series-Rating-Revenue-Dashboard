OTT Analytics: Web Series Rating & Revenue Dashboard

2 — Short description

This dashboard enables OTT industry analysts and platform managers to monitor web series performance at a glance. It aggregates data on viewer counts, total sales, profit, and ratings — broken down by industry (Bollywood, Hollywood, Tollywood, Chinese, Japanese), streaming platform, and genre — to surface which content categories and platforms drive the highest returns.

📊 4. Data Source

Source: Custom-generated dataset for web series analytics
The dataset contains detailed information about web series, including:
Date of release
Industry (Bollywood, Hollywood, etc.)

Ratings (1–5 scale)
Reviews (Poor, Average, Good, etc.)
OTT Platforms (Netflix, Amazon Prime, etc.)
Viewers (in millions)
Price per viewer
Total sales (₹ Crore)
Profit (₹ Crore)

5 — Features & highlights

Business problem

OTT platforms struggle to identify which industries and genres deliver the best return on investment. Without a unified view, decision-makers cannot easily compare platform performance or spot underperforming content categories.

Goal of the dashboard

Provide a single-screen view of web series performance across all industries, platforms, and genres so stakeholders can make data-driven content acquisition and investment decisions.

Key visuals

Industry-wise bar chart — compares total sales across Bollywood (₹890 Cr), Hollywood (₹1,848 Cr), Chinese (₹576 Cr), Japanese (₹702 Cr), and Tollywood (₹491 Cr).

Platform-wise profit chart — SonyLIV leads profit at ₹389 Cr, followed by Hotstar ₹363 Cr and JioCinema ₹355 Cr.

Genre-wise viewer count — Action (90 series, 4,558M viewers) and Drama (78 series, 4,337M viewers) dominate reach.

Top/Bottom 5 series by sales — Stranger Things leads at ₹37.36 Cr; low-rated series like Meteor Garden S2 trail at ₹0.77 Cr.

Monthly sales trend — Feb peaks at ₹887 Cr; Jan and Mar reflect moderate activity around ₹609–683 Cr.

Business impact & insights

Hollywood content generates 41% of total platform sales despite representing 29% of total series — signaling higher monetisation per title.

Thriller genre achieves the highest average rating (4.14) while Action has the highest volume — a signal to invest in quality Thriller content.

All five platforms maintain a near-equal average rating of ~4.0, but SonyLIV leads total series count (106) and profit — suggesting a broader, higher-performing catalogue.

6 — Key questions the dashboard answers

Which industry produces the most profitable web series, and which underperforms relative to its viewer base?

Which streaming platform has the highest viewer engagement and total revenue?

Which genre drives the most viewers, and does high viewer count always translate to high sales?

What are the top 5 and bottom 5 series by revenue, and what patterns (genre, platform, rating) do they share?

How does monthly sales trend vary — are there seasonal peaks or dips in content consumption?

Is there a correlation between rating (1–5) and total sales or viewer count?

7 — Conclusion

The Web Series Rating & Revenue Dashboard successfully consolidates 500 records into actionable intelligence. Hollywood content dominates revenue; Thriller is the highest-rated genre; SonyLIV leads platform profitability. The dashboard empowers content strategy teams to prioritise high-return genres and industries, renegotiate platform deals based on real performance data, and spot underperforming series before they drain further investment. With slicers and pivot-driven visuals, the tool is ready for dynamic exploration without requiring any advanced technical skill — making it a practical decision-support asset for OTT business analysts.
